HEAL
Health, Environment, Agriculture, and Labor Food Alliance

Vision


Our campaigns and our work are grounded in a shared vision for change: together, we are articulating what we really want, and the various tactics that will help us get there. To this end, we are co-creating a Real Food Platform, informed by the wisdom of member-based organizations and advocates that have deep experience and expertise in each of eleven planks.

To date, 34 organizations have provided content that shapes the platform. Now, we are asking for your input to assess the strength and feasibility of the solutions articulated. Below, you’ll find links to surveys for each of the platform planks.

Goal


For all people and all communities to have the right and the means to produce, procure, prepare, share, and eat food that’s nutritionally and culturally appropriate, free from exploitation of themselves and any other people, and to be in their full power in harmony with the rest of the natural world.

Campaigns

To achieve this goal we are organizing campaigns in three arenas

Community and TransLocal Organizing

Models of infrastructure and policy change that embody real solutions exist. HEAL Food Alliance will engage cross-community organizing (taking a "translocal" approach) to support cross-learning between community-run campaigns toward policies that address health, environment, agriculture, and labor. HEAL will also develop media to lift up these models. One example is the “Good Food Purchasing Policy” – a municipal procurement policy framework that we are supporting in multiple regions.

Clean Corporations

Our food system is controlled by a small handful of powerful corporations that have significant influence over politicians and the policies that govern health, environmental, agricultural, trade, and labor standards. Together, the Alliance will highlight the practices and policies that support businesses that adhere to the real food platform, and exposing the practices of and policies that enable the businesses that abuse workers, small farmers, consumer health, and the environment.

Political Leadership

Good governance of our food system depends on holding elected officials accountable to our Real Food Platform, and on having congressional champions of our work. One way that we will ensure this is through a partnership with Food Policy Action and Union of Concerned Scientists, working together on a call for a National Food Policy Agenda rooted in the Real Food Platform and championed by Congress members and presidential candidates.

About Us


With the input of dozens of leaders in the field, the HEAL (Health, Environment, Agriculture, and Labor) Food Alliance was founded by the Food Chain Workers Alliance, Movement Strategy Center, Real Food Generation, and the Union of Concerned Scientists.

Bringing together the various sectors of food & agriculture including farmers, farm & food service laborers, and eaters - with policy experts & community organizers & activists, HEAL seeks effective policy change in our national food system, from the corner store to Capitol Hill.

We share the belief that a renewed American food system will result in thriving local economies, better stewardship for the environment, and healthier individuals and families nationwide.

We invite you to co-create this emerging project by shaping a Real Food Platform that will ground our shared campaigns to come.
